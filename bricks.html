Awwal Bricks (First Class / Grade-A)

The premium choice for structural strength. Perfect rectangular shape, deep copper-red color, and highly moisture resistant. Best for load-bearing walls and exposed brickwork.

Doma Bricks (Second Class / Grade-B)

The smart choice for cost-effective strength. Slightly irregular shape but excellent structural durability. Best for interior partition walls and plastered masonry.

Pela Bricks (Third Class / Under-Burnt)

The budget option for non-load bearing work. Yellowish-orange color with high water absorption. Best for temporary structures, flooring bases, or ground leveling.

Tamda Bricks (Over-Burnt / Vitrified)

The heavy-duty water-resistant material. Dark purplish-black, extremely hard and dense. Best for foundations, waterlogged areas, and road aggregate.

How We Work

Clay to Building Site.

From digging the clay to dropping bricks at your gate — the whole process, explained simply.

01

We Start With Good Clay

We source high-quality clay from controlled sites nearby. It's mixed and prepared until it has the right consistency for shaping.

02

We Shape Every Brick the Same

Clay goes through a machine press that cuts each brick to exactly the same size. Consistency here means easier building for you later.

03

We Fire Them Properly

Bricks go into our continuous kiln and are fired at 900–1100°C for 48 to 72 hours. Temperature is watched closely throughout for even strength.

04

We Check, Then Deliver

Once cooled, bricks are inspected, sorted by grade, and loaded onto trucks. Delivered directly to your site within the time we agreed.
